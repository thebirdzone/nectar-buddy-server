DIY Nectar Recipe

A simple, safe nectar that hummingbirds love. No food coloring, no additives — just sugar and water.

Ingredients
White granulated sugar 1 part
Water 4 parts
Example: ¼ cup sugar + 1 cup water — scale up as needed.
Instructions
  1. 1Measure your sugar and water into a clean container or pitcher.
  2. 2Stir well until the sugar is completely dissolved. No heat needed.
  3. 3Pour into a clean feeder, or store the extra in an airtight container in the refrigerator.
🧊 Refrigerator storage: Homemade nectar keeps for up to 2 weeks in the fridge in a clean, sealed container. Label it with the date so you know when to make a fresh batch.
Avoid: honey, brown sugar, raw sugar, or any food coloring — these can be harmful to hummingbirds. Use only plain white granulated sugar.
